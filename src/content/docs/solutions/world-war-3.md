</style><svg style="display: none;"><defs id="MathJax_SVG_glyphs"></defs></svg><p>Imagine World War 3 began and the University of Moratuwa became a heavily fortified enemy base, with only one route remaining open through the Sumanadasa building.
This building is like a rectangular plot of land(<em><strong>HxW</strong></em>) represented by a xy coordinate plane (<em><strong>0 ≤ x ≤ W and 0 ≤ y ≤ H</strong></em>). The land is covered by four walls(x=0,y=0,x=W,y=H) and it is restricted to two gates. The Front Gate is in the middle of the front wall(y=0) and the Back Gate is in the middle of the back wall(y=H).</p>

<p>To keep watch on intruders, they've installed <em><strong>N</strong></em> number of CCTV cameras through the land. Each camera has a circular range region of radius <em><strong>R</strong></em> it can see, and they want to make sure no one sneaks from the Front Gate to the Back Gate without being spotted.</p>

<p>However, there's a bit of a loophole. Along the walls where the lines of trees are planted along the back wall(<em><strong>y=H</strong></em>) and the front wall(<em><strong>y=0</strong></em>). Therefore <em><strong>anyone can walk along the back and front walls without being detected by the cameras</strong></em>. However, some of these cameras may detect the left(x=0) and right(x=W) walls.</p>
